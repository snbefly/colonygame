+1

There is power in this soil, and truth in these hills. The people stole our lore, and made us forget the things our ancestors had learned. But the land is still talking to us all, and we can teach you how to listen.

Ancient Writings The code of life that your ancestors refined and put to written form are the law, the core and the boundary of your society.
Land You control a vast swath of the homeland, and you’re able to keep it safe and useable. Your living areas are spacious and roomy, and you have room to expand.
Recruits Your family is bustling with people. They may not be particularly skilled or handy, but in terms of raw manpower no-one comes close.
Scouts Your guards, patrols, or mapmakers ensure you’re well-informed about the surrounding area. Reports regularly come in on the geography and inhabitants of the land around you.
Trade You’re the focal point of a web of caravans and merchants that spans the homeland. You have your finger on the pulse of commerce, and collect a healthy skimming off the top.
Attunement The spirits of the forest lend your community assistance, and your ancestors visit in dreams to give you advice.
Prestige You are a threat and a boogeyman to the colonists even if you attempt to appear any different.
Safety You walk on a thin ice. Your borders are penetrated by florans, and everything that can go wrong will soon go wrong.

Doctrine

Keepers of the Land. Your Characters and Followers gain +1 to Defuse any environmental challenge or obstacle.

Lifestyle

Settled. Your buildings follow secret principles from Before, all long forgotten by everyone else. You gain Surplus: Defences and share it with those who live in your settlements.

Culture

Populace is a community of hermits preserving the old ways. Clothing is delicate robes. Governance is a council of elders

Special Moves

THE OZAKI WAY

Three things matter in life: to be true to yourself, to give more than you take and to leave no tainted footprints behind. As long as Druid character abides by these rules they gain 3-Armor versus natural hazards or injuries from acts of extreme athleticism in the wilds.

INNOVATIVE DISSENT

When you unleash a natural hazard, conduct a ritual to place Hostile Grounds on a section of the Homeland map. This hazard will last until the end of this Age. Determine the source of the Disadvantage and how you triggered said change. It will happen in a matter of days to weeks.

Special Moves

ATAVISTIC NATURE

Savagery - 1

Choose what gives your Family a ferocious edge in battle:

  • Superior senses and teamwork.
  • Superior physical attributes.
  • A feral, barely-restrained killing instinct.
Start with 3-Savagery. When you go all-out on an enemy, you may spend 1-Savagery to:
  • Separate your target from its allies.
  • Terrify your target with a display of violence.
  • Take out your target’s weakest units.
At the start of a new Age, reset to 3-Savagery

HOME. FAMILY. FUTURE.

When your Family fights for survival or supremacy, you can gain Need: Recruits, Morale and/or Motivation. For each you take, choose one:

  • One of your foe’s champions will be overwhelmed.
  • One chosen champion of your Family will decimate scores of your foe’s regular troops.
  • Those who witness the battle will be forever marked by the strength and power of your people.

Special Moves

GUERRILLA RADIO

When you broadcast your message far and wide, spend 2-Treaty on anyone with Surplus: Recruits to gain Surplus: Spies or Surplus: Scouts, located right there in their midst.

MIGHTY RENEGADES

It doesn’t matter that you are everyday people - together you will change the course of history. When you have one of Surplus: Recruits, Morale or Leadership and gain a second, you also get the third.

PANDORA'S BOX

When you delve deep into the planet's underground cave complex in search of something useful, describe what it is and what you want it for. The GM will give you 1-3 downsides, and then you can decide whether you still want to take it.

  • It’ll be useless after it is applied.
  • You’ll need help from another faction or character to use it well.
  • Something else follows you out.
  • The expedition takes its toll on the mining complex or its people
  • You are taking it away from an agreed buyer.
